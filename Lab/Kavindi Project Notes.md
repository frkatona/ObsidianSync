## history notes to self

- Polymer-grafted nanoparticles (PGNPs) 
	- interest originally grew from (1) surface modification of inorganic NPs for polymer nanocomposites and (2) polymer-brush chemistry on curved surfaces
- Polymer Derived Ceramics (PDCs)
	- go back to 50s polycarbosilane --> SiC
	- advantage: design of ceramic precursor allows control over composition, yield, cross-linking, srhinkage, and ceramic network/crystallinity
- PGNP + PDC --> incorporate NP into the ceramic network
- Martin et al (2020) on polycarbosilane-grafted NPs describes free-flowing hairy NP liquids which pyrolyze to ceramics
	- backbone structure and thermal treamtent affect rheology, NP-network formation, and ceramic yield
- 2023 paper extended to metal-free thiol-yne grafting to SiO2
- Posey, Delcamp, and Dickerson reported ionic-complexation-based PCD-PGNPs in 2023
	- cationic polycarbosilanes could be assembled with sulfonated silica, but only worked well if it was a modified 'hyperbranched' PCS, not the usual linear PCS
