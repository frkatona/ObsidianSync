# Interview Homework
### overall goal:
- prove you can lead a new product launch from kickoff to go-live with zero slip-ups. Your job is to simulate a 2-week sprint launch for a new Hadley Designs product (PreK Flashcards)—ensuring every team is aligned, every detail is caught, and every asset is delivered on time.  
- we want to see how you plan, communicate, QA, and execute when the stakes are high and timelines are tight.
- clear picture of how you think, communicate, and build systems that drive clarity, accountability, and high performance across our growing organization
- What We’re Evaluating:  
	- Your ability to plan, organize, and lead a high-pressure project
	- Your attention to detail and QA mindset
	- Your communication and team coordination skills
	- Your strategic thinking and improvement mindset

# Misc
## new software
- **Loom** (Atlassian's AI-assisted video capture software)
	- screen + audio capture + AI subtitles
- **Notion** (AI-assisted, collaborative word processor)
	- custom blocks/database personal design
	- cross-referencing pages
	- embed tasks inside research notes
- **ClickUp** (general project management)
	- task/issue tracking
		- task card assignment/status tracker, like Kanban + timeline/calendar
	- documentation centralization
		- built-in wiki system
		- SOPs, meeting notes, research notes, design specs, onboarding docs
	- collaboration/communication
		- simple chat
		- shared dashboards
		- commenting
		- mentions/notifications
	- workflow automation
		- rules like "when {status} changes --> notify {person}"
	- replaces
		- task/issue trackers (Trello, Asana, Todoist, Jira, Harvest, Gantt)
		- internal docs (Notion, Confluence)
		- spreadsheet trackers (Excel, Sheets)
	- does not replace
		- 'deep communication' tools (Slack, Teams)
		- source control/review (Github)
		- enterprise analytics systems
- **Airtable** (lightweight database and workflow engine)
	- building internal tools without coding
	- pipeline tracking (sales, experiments, content production)
	- limited in capability and scaling compared to SQL
		- when tables grow to ~50k+ records; high-frequency writes; millisecond retrieval; need utility from a transaction control language (TCL)
